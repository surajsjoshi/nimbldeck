Live Engagement

Bringing energy to your training sessions

Corporate training is way too dry and goes without much audience interaction. The result being, waste of time and money and poor ROI. Use nimblDeck to connect with your audience live during the training session. Create micro content for your training session like Q&A, polls and short lessons in few minutes. Keep the class engaged with these activities, keep them on them on their toes, and make sure they are actually learning during the training session.

Reinforcement

Helping trainees practice the skills they are learning

During traditional corporate training, participants rarely practice the skills they intend to learn. What happens when the session is over ? Everything goes down the drain. Use nimblDeck to create reinforcement exercises which the trainees can practice on their mobile app on the go. Understand the concepts or areas the trainees are lacking on.

Two way interaction

Facilitating query resolution and discussion for trainees

nimblDeck takes the standard one way flow of information and makes it possible for trainees to engage during the training by facilitating discussion. Using the mobile app trainees can post their views, queries or any kind of feedback for the trainer during the sessions or offline. This way the trainees would leave with clear recommendations, actionable tips, and concrete ways to handle situations on a regular basis. This back and forth way of teaching means they will take the skills learned and actually apply them to their job.

Real time Analytics

Understanding your audience better

Using nimblDeck dashboard gain deeper and real time insights into what the audience thinks with feedback on different viewpoints, their strengths and weaknesses of certain lesson concepts. With the web based dashboard, you can follow along to find real time insights from the audience such as:

Most challenging concepts.
Audience opinion distribution
Total audience engagement

Take these statistics and use them throughout the organization to better equip the next training session, or gain insight on the employee's thoughts and viewpoints on certain subjects.
